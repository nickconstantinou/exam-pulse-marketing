      <h2>Muscle Building Fundamentals</h2>

      <h3>Repetition Ranges</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><span class="timestamp">6-12 reps</span> â€” Optimal for hypertrophy (muscle growth)</li>
        <li><span class="timestamp">3-5 reps</span> â€” Strength focused</li>

      <h3>Sets and Frequency</h3>
      <p>For most women, <strong>10-20 sets per muscle group per week</strong> is the sweet spot. This can be broken into 2-3 sessions. Full-body workouts 2-3x per week are highly effective.</p>

      <h2>Nutrition for Women</h2>

      <h3>Protein</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Aim for <strong>1.6-2.2g per kg</strong> of body weight daily</li>
        <li>Protein timing matters less than total daily intake</li>
        <li>Creatine monohydrate: <strong>3-5g daily</strong> â€” works equally well for women</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Key Supplements</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Creatine:</strong> 3-5g daily (safe for women, supports muscle and brain health)</li>
        <li><strong>Vitamin D:</strong> If deficient, supplement accordingly</li>
        <li><strong>Omega-3s:</strong> If not eating fatty fish regularly</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Training Through the Cycle</h2>

      <p>Research shows training adaptations can vary across the menstrual cycle. Some women may feel stronger during the follicular phase and more fatigued during menstruation. Listen to your body and adjust intensity accordingly.</p>

      <h3>Perimenopause & Menopause</h3>
      <p>Women lose muscle mass faster during menopause due to hormonal changes. Resistance training becomes crucial. Focus on:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Progressive overload (slow progression)</li>
        <li>Higher protein intake</li>
        <li>Consistent training (2-3x per week minimum)</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Common Myths Debunked</h2>

      <h3>"Women will get too bulky"</h3>
      <p><span class="highlight">False.</span> Women have 15-20x less testosterone than men, making it physiologically very difficult to build large muscles. Resistance training will make women stronger and more toned.</p>

      <h3>"Training fasted is better"</h3>
      <p>Not necessarily. Most research shows no significant difference in fat loss between fasted and fed training. If you train better after eating, eat before. Performance comes first.</p>

      <h3>"You need different exercises for women"</h3>
      <p>The fundamental movements are the same: push, pull, hinge, squat, carry. Exercise selection should be based on goals and individual biomechanics, not gender.</p>

      <h2>Cardio & The Interference Effect</h2>

      <p>Too much cardio can interfere with strength gains (the "interference effect"). For women looking to build muscle:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Prioritize strength training 2-3x per week</li>
        <li>Add cardio after lifting or on separate days</li>
        <li>Walking is excellent and low-stress</li>
        <li>1-2 HIIT sessions per week can be beneficial</li>
      </ul>
